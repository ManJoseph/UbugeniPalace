# Project Overview: UbugeniPalace (U-Pal) 🎨

UbugeniPalace is a dedicated e-commerce marketplace built to showcase authentic Rwandan craftsmanship to the world. It provides a digital platform where local artisans can tell their stories, showcase their work, and connect directly with global art lovers.

## 🏺 Purpose & Vision
Rwanda is home to incredible artisan talent—from traditional Agaseke basket weaving to modern painting and sculpture. Many of these artists have limited access to the global market. UbugeniPalace was created to:
- **Empower Local Talent:** Providing a professional digital presence for artisans.
- **Celebrate Culture:** Highlighting the traditional techniques and cultural heritage behind every piece.
- **Enable Sustainable Growth:** Helping artisans build sustainable livelihoods by reaching a broader audience.

## 🚀 Key Features for Users

### For Customers
- **Discovery:** Browse and filter unique handmade treasures by category, price, and location.
- **Artisan Stories:** Each artisan has a dedicated profile to share their journey and passion.
- **Seamless Experience:** Clean URLs and responsive design for easy navigation on any device.
- **Secure Shopping:** Dedicated wishlist and cart system for a smooth shopping journey.

### For Artisans
- **Digital Storefront:** A personalized dashboard to manage products and showcase their craft.
- **Direct Control:** Artisans can upload their own products, update prices, and manage inventory.
- **Community:** Joining a growing network of Rwandan craftspeople.

## 💎 Modern Features Implemented (March 2026)
We've recently upgraded the platform to be more stable, professional, and future-proof:
- **Clean Navigation:** No more `.php` in URLs, giving the site a modern app-like feel.
- **Cloud Hosting:** Powered by Supabase and Cloudinary for enterprise-grade performance.
- **Reliable Infrastructure:** Dockerized setup ensures the app is easy to deploy and maintain.
- **Graceful Error Handling:** Custom branded failure pages to ensure users are never left with a broken experience.
